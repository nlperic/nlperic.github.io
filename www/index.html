About me

I am an Associate Professor (tenured in 2021) in School of Transport and Logistics at the Southwest Jiaotong University (SWJTU), China. Before taking this position, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at Tokyo Institute of Technology for two and a half years. I obtained Ph.D. degree in Civil Engineering (Transportation) from the same institution in 2017. Both my postdoctoral and doctoral trainings were advised by Prof. Daisuke Fukuda.

Research interests

I lead the Transportation System and Urban Network Analysis (TUNA) Lab in SWJTU. The TUNA Lab focuses on a variety of optimization problems and equilibrium models in logistics and transportation networks. In particular, we leverage operations research, game theory, and machine learning tools to design mechanisms and algorithms for equitable and efficient transportation systems.
Some specific directions that we currently focus on include

  • Interpretable traffic flow theory and dynamic traffic assignment models

  • Data-driven transportation network optimization models and algorithms

  • Mechanism design for emerging transportation technologies
